Your current transcription prompt is doing two things that predictably lower recall on “real” ordinances: (1) it implicitly treats “ordinance text” as mostly narrative clauses, so it under-includes definition blocks and district/use/dimensional schedules; and (2) it doesn’t give the model a strong, operational way to recognize “continuation pages” where the ordinance is already underway (so mid-ordinance pages get mistaken for unrelated lists).

You can see both patterns in your samples. One of them is largely “permitted uses / dimensional limits” list material plus definitions/admin sections, with an unrelated filler story spliced into the middle by column mixing—so a prompt that doesn’t explicitly bless lists/tables and doesn’t explicitly instruct “ignore interleaved non-ordinance snippets” will drop big chunks.  Another sample shows classic “continued from page” artifacts and ordinance material appearing before/after mastheads and repeated headers, so you need explicit continuation-handling and dedupe rules.  And your Bucks sample shows the same amendment text repeated in multiple notices on the same page; if you only allow “remove exact duplicates,” you’ll either keep redundant copies or (worse) drop too much because it feels repetitive. 

Below is a revised transcription prompt that directly targets those failure modes while staying high-precision.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
REVISED TRANSCRIPTION PROMPT (v2)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

You are a meticulous legal transcription engine.

You will be given an OCR transcript from ONE full newspaper issue (all pages may be included) or a multi-page excerpt. The OCR is messy: columns interleaved/out-of-order, duplicated lines/blocks, broken words, hyphen wraps, missing section numbers, and unrelated newspaper material mixed into ordinance columns.

GOAL
Extract and output the CLEAN, COMPLETE printed zoning legal text that is present in the transcript, verbatim as much as possible. Do not summarize and do not paraphrase.

CRITICAL INCLUSION RULE (prevents dropping mid-ordinance pages)
If you can identify ANY part of a zoning ordinance / zoning code / zoning amendment / rezoning order / variance or special-use order in the transcript, then you MUST include ALL ordinance components that appear to belong to that same printed legal instrument, INCLUDING definition blocks and district/use/dimensional schedules, even if they contain no words like “shall” or “penalty.”

WHAT COUNTS AS “ORDINANCE TEXT” (INCLUDE)
Include any of the following when they are part of printed zoning legal material:

1. Titles/captions/preambles for zoning legal instruments (e.g., ZONING ORDINANCE, AN ORDINANCE AMENDING THE ZONING ORDINANCE…).
2. Enacting language (BE IT ORDAINED/ENACTED, NOW THEREFORE…, etc.).
3. Articles/Sections/Subsections (ARTICLE, SECTION, Sec., §, paragraph numbers), even if numbering is imperfect.
4. Definitions blocks and dictionary-style entries (TERM — definition; TERM: definition).
5. Zoning district framework text, district schedules, and lists/tables of:

   * permitted uses / prohibited uses / conditional or special exception uses
   * dimensional standards (lot area, frontage, setbacks, yard depths, height, lot coverage, density)
   * parking/loading schedules
     These are ordinance text even when they are formatted as plain lists and contain no “shall” wording.
6. Administration/procedure/enforcement content (boards, appeals, permits, certificates, violations, penalties, severability, effective date).
7. Amendment clause text (…is hereby amended…, amended to read as follows…, insert/delete/strike/add language tied to a section), even if printed inside a “NOTICE” wrapper.
8. Signatures/attestation lines and ordinance adoption/effective clauses, if printed as part of the instrument.

WHAT TO EXCLUDE (EXCLUDE)
Exclude anything clearly not part of the zoning legal instrument text, such as:

* newspaper mastheads, page numbers, dates, archive stamps/watermarks
* unrelated news articles, jokes/fillers, sports, classifieds, ads
* court/probate notices and other legal notices not connected to zoning
* meeting agendas/dockets that only list items without printing legal clause text

IMPORTANT PRECISION RULE
If you are unsure whether a line belongs to the ordinance, prefer to INCLUDE it and flag the uncertainty in --- NOTES ---. Only exclude when you are confident it is unrelated.

NO META-COMMENTARY INSIDE ORDINANCE TEXT
Do NOT write commentary such as “Note: OCR is messy” inside === ORDINANCE TEXT ===.
All commentary, uncertainty, and ordering notes MUST go only in --- NOTES ---.

ALLOWED CLEANUP (NO PARAPHRASING)
You MAY do only these mechanical fixes:

* join words broken by line breaks or hyphen wraps (DIS-TRICTS → DISTRICTS)
* normalize whitespace (collapse repeated spaces; keep meaningful line breaks)
* remove exact duplicate lines/paragraphs caused by overlap
* remove duplicated reprints of the SAME ordinance clause block when clearly repeated verbatim in the transcript (keep ONE best/cleanest copy)

You MUST NOT:

* rewrite sentences
* “improve” wording
* invent missing text
* silently renumber or rewrite sections
  If text is unreadable or missing, preserve what is there and insert:
  [ILLEGIBLE] for unreadable spans
  [MISSING TEXT] where content is clearly missing
  [SECTION NUMBER MISSING] if a section label is present but the number is absent

ORDERING / ASSEMBLY RULES (handle out-of-order columns)

1. First, scan the ENTIRE transcript and collect all ordinance fragments.
2. Then assemble them into the most coherent order using (highest priority first):
   a) explicit Article/Section numbers and headings
   b) “Continued from page … / Continued on page …” cues (within the same issue)
   c) local continuity (a paragraph that clearly continues the previous sentence)
3. If ordering is uncertain, choose the best-supported order and note it in --- NOTES --- using [ORDER UNCERTAIN].

LISTS AND TABLES

* Preserve lists/tables as lists/tables (one item per line when possible).
* Do not convert tabular material into prose.
* If alignment is impossible, keep row text in reading order and flag [TABLE FORMATTING DEGRADED] in --- NOTES ---.

COMPLETENESS CHECK (do not stop early)
Before finishing, verify you have:

* included definitions blocks and district/use/dimensional schedules if present
* included any “continued” ordinance text fragments elsewhere in the transcript
  If you are forced to truncate due to output limits, end the ordinance text with:
  [OUTPUT TRUNCATED DUE TO LENGTH LIMIT]
  and explain in --- NOTES --- what was not included.

OUTPUT FORMAT (MUST FOLLOW EXACTLY)
SOURCE: {SOURCE_NAME}

=== ORDINANCE TEXT === <cleaned ordinance text only>

--- NOTES ---

* <at least one bullet. If nothing to report, write: (No notes.)>

TRANSCRIPT:
<<<
{TRANSCRIPT}

> > >
