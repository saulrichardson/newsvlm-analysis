Your job is to reconstruct the cleanest, most accurate operative zoning ordinance text you can from this issue.

SOURCE OF TRUTH
- The page images are the primary evidence.
- The OCR issue transcript is a noisy draft.
- When OCR and image disagree, prefer the image.
- Use the OCR transcript to find candidate ordinance text quickly, then use the images to repair, order, and structure it.
- Use only evidence available within this issue: the attached images, the OCR transcript, visible numbering sequence, and repeated local wording. Do not rely on outside ordinance templates or external legal knowledge.

HIGH-LEVEL TASK
Recover the operative zoning ordinance text in THIS ISSUE and present it as a clean, structured legal transcription.

If the ordinance spans multiple pages, combine those pages into one coherent transcription in reading order.
If only part of the issue is relevant, ignore unrelated pages and unrelated material on otherwise relevant pages.

This is a reconstruction task.
You should actively repair OCR damage, restore structure, fix column order, and resolve broken numbering when the attached images provide strong evidence.

PAGE ALIGNMENT
- Use the page image order list and issue transcript page markers to align transcript pages with the attached images.
- Printed newspaper continuation labels such as Continued from Page 23 may refer to the newspaper's internal pagination, not the issue page numbers or page_ids in the transcript.
- For alignment, trust the supplied issue page order and attached-image order.

RECONSTRUCTION STANDARD
You may make strong repairs when the images plus local context provide strong evidence.

You SHOULD:
- identify which issue pages actually contain the operative zoning ordinance text
- combine ordinance text across pages when the ordinance continues
- fix broken column order
- reassemble split clauses across column and page wraps
- restore missing digits, letters, hyphens, punctuation, and short words
- restore section numbers, subsection numbering, item letters, and ordinance titles when supported by visible text and sequence
- restore ordinance numbers, jurisdiction lines, article headings, enacting clauses, effective-date language, passage blocks, and attestation blocks when strongly evidenced by the images
- recover readable captions, labels, explanatory prose, and table-adjacent notes that are visibly part of the ordinance
- normalize formatting into readable ordinance text

You MAY use standard ordinance pattern completion for short repairs only when all of the following are true:
1. the relevant page image visibly supports the clause structure,
2. most of the relevant words are present or legible,
3. the remaining repair is narrow and formulaic,
4. the repaired wording is strongly constrained by nearby text within this same issue.

TABLES, DIAGRAMS, AND GRAPHIC REGIONS
- If a table, chart, or diagram contains readable ordinance text, captions, labels, headings, or explanatory notes, include that readable text.
- If surrounding prose clearly refers to the graphic and the visible text supports it, preserve that prose.
- If a numeric rule, threshold, or dimensional standard is clearly readable from the image, you may reconstruct it cleanly into the surrounding ordinance text.
- Do NOT invent unreadable table cells, numerical grids, or long graphic content that cannot actually be read from the images.
- If a graphic clearly contains operative material but part is not recoverable, preserve the readable parts and mark the unreadable span with [ILLEGIBLE] or [MISSING TEXT] as appropriate.

BOUNDARIES
- Include only the operative zoning ordinance text.
- If the ordinance ends mid-page and unrelated notices, ads, court items, bids, obituaries, or other ordinances begin after it, stop at the ordinance boundary.
- Treat a caption, summary, continuation stub, or legal notice header alone as insufficient ordinance text.

You MUST NOT:
- fabricate large unseen passages
- merge unrelated non-zoning ordinances into the transcription
- include narrative summaries, hearing logistics, or non-operative discussion unless they are part of the printed operative ordinance text itself
- paraphrase graphics into new legal prose unless the printed words are actually visible and strongly supported

GAP MARKING
- If part of a clause is still not recoverable after using both OCR and image evidence, preserve as much as you can and mark the gap with [ILLEGIBLE].
- If a page clearly continues ordinance text but a crucial span is entirely absent from both OCR and image evidence, use [MISSING TEXT].
